ParkPeer Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 19, 2026
Last updated: July 19, 2026

ParkPeer (“ParkPeer”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) is a community parking application that helps drivers share information about parking spaces, request and manage parking reservations, and coordinate a parking handover.

This Privacy Policy explains what information ParkPeer processes, why it is processed, how it is protected, when it may be shared, how long it is retained, and the choices available to users.

  • Developer: Aleksandar Nenadovic
  • Application: ParkPeer
  • Android package: com.parkpeer.app
  • Country: Greece
  • Privacy and support email: ParkPer.dev@gmail.com

For privacy questions, requests, or complaints, contact us at ParkPer.dev@gmail.com.

2. Information ParkPeer processes

Depending on the features you use, ParkPeer may process the following categories of information.

2.1 Account and identity information

  • Firebase user identifier;
  • verified telephone number;
  • display name and initials;
  • linked Google account email address when Google recovery is enabled;
  • account creation and last sign-in timestamps;
  • authentication provider and security-session information.

ParkPeer does not receive or store your Google password. Google authentication is handled by Google and Firebase Authentication.

2.2 Vehicle information

  • vehicle brand, model, type, and colour;
  • a licence-plate value if you choose to provide it;
  • selected or active vehicle.

Where a plate is shown to another user during a parking exchange, ParkPeer is designed to show only a masked value, such as the final characters, rather than the complete plate.

2.3 Precise and approximate location

ParkPeer may process device location when you grant location permission, including:

  • the position of a parking space you publish;
  • your position relative to nearby parking spaces;
  • the saved position of your own parked vehicle;
  • live driver position, distance, estimated arrival time, and location freshness during an accepted reservation;
  • location used to determine arrival, confirm a parking result, prevent misuse, and resolve an exchange;
  • coarse area, geohash, time, and signal-quality information used for parking prediction and service improvement.

Live location is used only for the relevant parking flow and is stopped when the exchange ends, is cancelled, or the user signs out. Location access can be disabled in Android settings, but location-dependent features will no longer work correctly.

2.4 Parking, reservation, and community activity

  • published parking availability and planned availability time;
  • reservation requests and decisions;
  • parking-space owner and driver roles;
  • wait-extension requests and responses;
  • parking confirmations, occupied-space reports, cancellations, and failed exchanges;
  • timestamps, distance, estimated arrival time, and exchange status;
  • ratings, reputation, trust indicators, restrictions, and anti-abuse incidents;
  • notifications relating to an active parking flow.

2.5 P balance and transaction information

ParkPeer processes the user’s virtual P balance, reserved P amounts, rewards, penalties, and transaction history required for the in-app parking economy.

P is an in-app virtual unit used by ParkPeer. It is not a bank account, cryptocurrency, stored monetary value, or independently transferable legal tender.

2.6 Free, Trial, and Pro membership

ParkPeer may offer Free access, a limited free Trial, and paid Pro membership. To manage access, ParkPeer may process membership tier, entitlement status, trial start/expiry, subscription product identifier, purchase token or transaction reference, purchase/renewal/expiry time, cancellation state, and verification result.

Purchases and subscriptions are processed by Google Play Billing. ParkPeer does not receive or store full card or bank-account details. Google may process payment, billing, tax, fraud-prevention, and transaction data under its own policies.

Deleting a ParkPeer account or uninstalling the app does not automatically cancel an active Google Play subscription. Cancel it separately through Google Play → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions → ParkPeer. Limited transaction references may be retained for verification, refunds, fraud prevention, accounting, or legal compliance.

2.7 Device, application, and security information

  • Firebase Cloud Messaging registration token;
  • app version, Android version, device manufacturer and model;
  • language, country, timezone, and basic application configuration;
  • Firebase App Check and Play Integrity signals used to determine whether requests originate from a genuine installation;
  • technical events needed for reliability, fraud prevention, abuse investigation, and security.

2.8 Privacy-preserving parking analytics

ParkPeer may store parking prediction events containing location area, event time, parking action, app/device characteristics, GPS accuracy, and signal-quality measurements. These events are designed to use an anonymous installation identifier rather than the user’s ParkPeer account identity and have a stated retention period of up to 365 days.

Despite this design, location and device information can be sensitive. We use these events only for parking prediction, product analytics, quality improvement, safety, and fraud prevention.

3. How information is collected

Information is collected:

  • directly from you when you register, edit a profile, add a vehicle, publish parking, make a reservation, report an outcome, or contact support;
  • automatically from your device when you grant permissions or use features requiring location, messaging, integrity checks, or technical diagnostics;
  • from other ParkPeer users participating in the same parking exchange;
  • from Google and Firebase when you authenticate, link account recovery, receive notifications, or use map and location services.

4. Purposes of processing

ParkPeer processes information to:

  1. create, authenticate, secure, recover, and maintain your account;
  2. provide the parking map, publishing, reservation, Smart search, and handover flows;
  3. display relevant vehicle and arrival information to the other participant in an accepted exchange;
  4. maintain the P balance, rewards, refunds, holds, and penalties;
  5. send reservation and security notifications;
  6. calculate distance, estimated arrival, arrival confirmation, and parking availability;
  7. detect duplicate actions, fraud, manipulation, abusive reports, and unsafe behaviour;
  8. enforce temporary restrictions or account blocks where necessary to protect users;
  9. create aggregated or de-identified statistics and improve parking predictions;
  10. maintain, debug, secure, and improve the application;
  11. comply with applicable legal obligations and respond to valid legal requests.

5. Legal bases for EEA/UK users

Where the GDPR or UK GDPR applies, the relevant legal bases may include:

  • Performance of a contract: providing the account and requested parking functionality;
  • Consent: location permission, notifications where required, optional Google recovery, and optional profile/vehicle information;
  • Legitimate interests: service security, fraud prevention, abuse detection, reliability, support, and carefully limited product improvement;
  • Legal obligation: retaining or disclosing information where required by applicable law.

You may withdraw consent through Android permissions, application settings, or by contacting us. Withdrawal does not affect processing already lawfully completed.

6. When information is visible to other users

ParkPeer is a community exchange service. Information necessary to coordinate a parking exchange may be shown to the relevant participant, including:

  • display name or initials;
  • reputation or trust information;
  • relevant vehicle description and masked plate;
  • parking-space location;
  • reservation status, distance, estimated arrival, and live driver movement during an accepted reservation;
  • wait-extension and parking-result decisions.

ParkPeer does not intend to publish your telephone number or Google email address to other users.

7. Service providers and data sharing

ParkPeer uses service providers that process information on our behalf or as independent providers, including:

  • Google Firebase: Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Cloud Functions, Cloud Messaging, and App Check;
  • Google Play services: device integrity, Google sign-in/credential services, location services, and application distribution;
  • Google Maps Platform / Google Maps app: map display and optional external navigation.

These providers process information under their own terms and privacy documentation. ParkPeer may also disclose information:

  • when required by law, court order, or a valid government request;
  • to investigate fraud, abuse, threats, or security incidents;
  • to protect the rights and safety of ParkPeer, its users, or others;
  • as part of a merger, acquisition, financing, or transfer of the service, subject to appropriate safeguards and notice where required.

ParkPeer does not sell personal information. ParkPeer does not share personal information for third-party behavioural advertising.

8. International processing

Google and other service providers may process information in countries outside your country of residence. Where required, transfers are subject to contractual and legal safeguards provided by the relevant service provider and applicable law.

9. Data retention

We retain information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described above:

  • account, profile, vehicle, active parking, notification, and P-wallet data are retained while the account is active;
  • temporary Smart sessions, matches, live tracking, reservations, and waiting requests are closed or removed when they expire or the flow ends, subject to operational cleanup schedules;
  • completed parking and trust events may be retained in de-identified form for safety, fraud prevention, statistics, and service quality;
  • privacy-preserving parking prediction analytics may be retained for up to 365 days;
  • limited security records may be retained when reasonably necessary to investigate fraud, prevent abuse, resolve disputes, or comply with law.

Retention may be extended where required for an active investigation, legal claim, or legal obligation. When retention is no longer necessary, data is deleted or de-identified.

10. Account and data deletion

Delete inside the application

In ParkPeer, open:

Profile → Account security → Permanently delete ParkPeer account

The application asks you to reconfirm the linked Google account. Deletion is unavailable while an active parking exchange is in progress, because the exchange must first be safely completed or cancelled.

Account deletion removes the Firebase Authentication account and associated active profile data, including vehicles, notifications, P wallet transactions, reservation blocks, active parking records, Smart sessions, and Smart matches. Historical safety and parking records may be retained only after personal identifiers are replaced or removed, as described in this policy.

Request deletion outside the application

If you no longer have the application or cannot access your account, email ParkPer.dev@gmail.com with the subject:

ParkPeer account deletion request

Include the telephone number associated with the account and enough information for us to verify ownership. Do not send an SMS verification code, Google password, or other password by email. We may request a safe identity-verification step before completing deletion.

We will acknowledge and complete a valid request within a reasonably necessary period, subject to identity verification, active-exchange resolution, security requirements, and applicable law.

12. Permissions and device controls

ParkPeer may request:

  • Precise or approximate location: maps, nearby parking, parking publication, saved-car location, reservation tracking, arrival, confirmation, and fraud prevention;
  • Background/foreground-service location: live driver tracking during an accepted reservation;
  • Notifications: reservation, parking, waiting, arrival, and account-related notices.

Permissions can be changed in Android Settings. Disabling a permission limits the related feature.

13. Security

ParkPeer uses Firebase Authentication, Firestore Security Rules, protected Cloud Functions, Firebase App Check, Play Integrity for release builds, access controls, masked vehicle identifiers, server-side validation, and encrypted network connections provided by Google services.

No system can guarantee absolute security. Users should protect their device, telephone number, Google account, and verification codes, and should use Sign out all devices immediately if a device is lost or stolen.
